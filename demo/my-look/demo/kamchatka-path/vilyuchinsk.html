volcano Vilyuchinsky

Vilyuchik or Vilyuchinsky - Volcano in Kamchatka, located south-west of Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, behind Avacha bay. It is located on the watershed Vilyucha and a large barn in the upper reaches of the river Paratunka.

The volcano is an extinct stratovolcano, is represented by a regular cone height of 2175 m above sea level.

The top of the volcano is cut off to the west and looks like a large rock outcrops separated by an accumulation of ice and firn. The lava and pyroclastics, composed of the remnants from the surface changed to a mottled appearance, possibly due to the impact of fumaroles in the recent past. The slopes of the volcano are cut by radiating from the top of a deep barranco, and some of them originate from the middle of the slope. Cavities in the upper north-western slope filled with ice and firn. Fold the volcano lava and pyroclastics of andesite-basalt composition. At the bottom are placed olivine basalts above - augite and plagioclase-hornblende-hypersthene andesites. On the eastern slope of the volcano there are traces of ancient solfataras [2], and hot springs are located in the southeastern part of the volcano. Repeatedly observed gas-vapor discharge on its top. At the bottom of the cone is a picturesque waterfall.
